Detailed Description

A SymmetricCylinder represents a cylindrical surface that can have a varying radius along its length. Unlike a Cylinder, however, its central axis cannot be bent and must be a straight line, and the radius must be radially constant, i.e. the radius can only change along the length of the cylinder, but not along the radial axis. These properties make this surface perfect to represent a hinge joint surface. Due to its simplicity, it is raytraceable.

Construct a pie-slice of a new SymmetricCylinder around a central axis, the centerLine. The radius at each point on the central axis is defined by a one-dimensional function radius. The cylinder is cut in a pie-slice along the length of the shaft, defined by the angles startAngle and endAngle.

The surface is parametrized with the coordinates \(t \in [0, 1]\) (along the length of the shaft) and \(\phi \in [0, 2 \pi]\) (along the radial coordinate).

This gives a pie-sliced cylindrical surface that is defined only between the angles startAngle and endAngle. For example, if startAngle = 0.0f and endAngle = Math.PI, one would get a cylinder that is sliced in half.
